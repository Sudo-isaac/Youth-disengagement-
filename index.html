The Challenge

Why Are Rockingham's
Youth in Crisis?

Across Rockingham, thousands of young people have limited access to safe, free, and engaging spaces. The consequences are playing out on our streets every week.

Rising Crime Statistics

Youth-related incidents in Rockingham have climbed significantly, including vandalism, theft, antisocial behaviour, and vehicle offences — many happening in the after-school hours.

↑34%

Nothing to Do After School

With limited free activities, youth are left unsupervised for 5–6 hours each day. Boredom is one of the strongest predictors of antisocial behaviour in adolescents.

5–6 hrs

Social Disconnection

Young people without structured social outlets struggle to form positive peer groups. Disengagement from school and community leads directly to higher risk of criminal involvement.

1 in 3

Digital Isolation & Mental Health

Without real-world activities, many young people fall into excessive screen time, social media loops, and mental health deterioration — creating a cycle of disengagement.

↑41%

Lack of Infrastructure

Rockingham has significant residential growth, yet youth-focused public infrastructure hasn't kept pace. The ratio of free recreational facilities to young residents remains critically low.

Critical

Cost Barriers to Activities

Private gyms, sports clubs, and entertainment venues are unaffordable for many local families. Without free alternatives, lower-income youth are disproportionately excluded from healthy activities.

How We Got Here

A Problem Years
in the Making

The rise in youth disengagement and crime isn't sudden — it's the result of years of unmet needs in a growing community.

Root Cause

Rapid population growth, slow infrastructure

Rockingham's population surged over the past decade, drawing families to its affordable suburbs. But youth-focused public spaces and free activities failed to scale with demand. The ratio of young residents to recreational facilities grew increasingly lopsided.

Escalation

Boredom becomes a daily reality

With limited transport options, no central youth hub, and cost barriers to paid activities, thousands of young people found themselves with hours of unstructured time and nowhere constructive to direct their energy. Hanging around carparks, malls, and streets became the norm.

Present

Crime and antisocial behaviour spike

Community reports of vandalism, vehicle theft, antisocial behaviour, and harassment have increased steadily. Local residents feel less safe. Businesses are suffering. And the young people involved are often not bad kids — they're bored, disengaged, and desperate for stimulation.

Opportunity

There is a proven solution — and it starts here

Evidence from communities across Australia and internationally is clear: when you give young people free, engaging, and accessible spaces to be active and social, crime drops. We have the opportunity to be that community. The question is whether we act now.

Our Proposal

Give Them Somewhere
to Be.

The fix isn't more policing. It's more possibility. Here's what we're calling for.

Boredom is the Problem.
Parks, Pipes & Purpose are the Answer.

We're asking the City of Rockingham and the WA Government to invest in youth infrastructure — starting with a dedicated skate and bike park, and expanding free structured activities across the region.

⭐ Priority Project

The Rockingham Bike & Skate Park

A dedicated, free-access bike, skate, and BMX park in a central Rockingham location. Designed for all skill levels, open to all ages, and built to bring youth together in a supervised, energised environment.

  • Free, open-access 7 days a week
  • BMX, skateboard, scooter & mountain bike zones
  • Lit for evening use — keeping youth visible and safe
  • Youth-led design input from local riders
  • Community coaching & events program
  • Estimated cost: $800K–$1.2M (vs $2.1M annual crime cost)
Idea

Free After-School Sports Programs

Subsidised or free entry to local sporting clubs, gyms, and group activities for youth aged 10–18. Coordinated through schools and community organisations, with transport assistance.

Idea

Youth Art & Music Spaces

A dedicated creative hub — somewhere for young people to record music, make art, run events, and express themselves. Creativity is one of the most powerful protective factors against disengagement.

Idea

Pop-Up Events & Street Activation

Regular free community events — markets, skateboard competitions, music nights — that activate public spaces and bring youth into positive community experiences.

Idea

Youth Mentorship Network

Connecting at-risk young people with trained local mentors, ex-athletes, and community leaders who can provide guidance, accountability, and positive role modelling.

Idea

Extended Library & Community Centre Hours

Open our existing facilities later into the evening, providing safe, supervised, and free spaces for homework, gaming, socialising, and quiet time.

Priority

Youth Advisory Board

Give young people a genuine seat at the table. Create a youth-led advisory body that influences council decisions on recreation, transport, and community investment. Ownership builds investment.
